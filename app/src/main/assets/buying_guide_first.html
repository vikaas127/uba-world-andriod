A

Auction - A public sale in which property or items of merchandise are sold to the highest bidder.

B

Buildings Insurance - No it's not a way to get free money. This type of Insurance is against the cost of repair or rebuilding a property from scratch following structural damage, for example by flood, fire or storm.

Building survey - This means a full survey on the condition of your property.

C

Chain - We are not talking about the bling around your neck. In property world, a chain is when a line of buyers and sellers are all linked together because each is selling and buying a property from one of the others, apart from the people at the beginning and at the end of the chain.

Completion date - This is the date where you post on Instagram 'started from the bottom, now we're here' Hashtag Homeowners!! This is the date when you are able to move into your new home.

Conveyancer - A qualified advisor of the legal requirements of buying or selling a property.

Conveyancing - Legal paperwork in which you're moving the legal ownership of property or land from one person to another.

D

Deeds - Legal documents assigning ownership of a property and/or land.

Deposit - Feels like you are forever saving for this! This is the amount you put towards the cost of the property when you buy your home.

E

Equity - This type is only fair when your property increases in value. It is when you own a property and the value of the property in the current market is worth more than what the property was bought for. You then have a valuable asset = equity.

Exchange of Contracts - The point at which the sale becomes legally binding from which neither party can withdraw without financial penalties

F

Fixed Price - The cue to negotiate. Means offers are invited at the price shown.

Freehold – When you don't owe anyone rent for the rest of your life! If you own the freehold, it means that you own the building and the land it stands on outright, in perpetuity. It is your name in the land registry as “freeholder”, owning the “title absolute”.

FTB - First time buyer.

G

Gazumping - When a buyer has had an offer to purchase a property accepted by the seller, but before the sale is completed the seller accepts a better offer from another buyer. Thats got to hurt!

Ground rent - Who would have thought you have to pay for the ground you walk on! The annual fee which a leaseholder pays to a freeholder as the property is situated on freeholders land.

Guarantor - A guardian angel! HONESTLY! Actually someone that agrees to pay the borrower's debt if the borrower defaults on the loan.

H

HMO - I know it sounds like something you have to pay for. I am afraid it is, if you need one. It stands for House in Multiple Occupations. You will need an HMO license when a residential property is occupied by:

  • 3 or more people who are unrelated and living there as at least 2 separate households.
  • The 3 or more people living there share a bathroom and/or kitchen.
I

Instruction - When a seller instructs an estate agent to market a property.

J

Joint Mortgage - A mortgage where there is more than one individual named responsible for the mortgage.

L

Land Registry - The Government organisation that holds records of all registered properties in England and Wales.

Lease - A contract for the occupation of a property.

Leasehold – It means that you just have a lease from the freeholder (sometimes called the landlord) to use the home for a number of years. The leases are usually long term – often 90 years or 120 years and as high as 999 years – but can be short, such as 40 years.

M

Maintenance Charge - A charge made towards the upkeep of a leasehold property.

Mortgage - an agreement between you and a bank or a similar organisation to borrow money to buy a house.

O

Off-plan - Selling a property without leaving work. It might as well be that but it actually means buying a property before it has been built.

R

Reassignment - When you buy a new build from a vendor rather than the developer directly.

Re-mortgage - This occurs when a property is used to finance a second mortgage if the property has risen in value since the initial mortgage was agreed. Otherwise, this describes the transfer of an existing mortgage to a new lender.

S

Service charge - A charge for maintenance and insurance of communal areas, which is normally only applicable to flats.

Share of freehold - where the freehold is split jointly between a number of flat owners within the property and the freehold is held in their personal names. This can be done by up to four flat owners. The second is when a company owns the freehold and each of the tenants holds a share (sometimes referred to as a membership) of said company.

Sole agency - This means an agreement to sell your property through one agent only, usually for a fixed period e.g. 12 weeks.

Solicitor - Legal expert handling all documentation for the sale and purchase of a property.

Stamp Duty - Money Money Money!!! It is a tax paid to the Government by the buyer upon completion.

Subject to Contract -The easy way to indicate that an agreement is not yet legally Binding without actually saying it.

T

Tender - selling property through advertising media designed to capture the maximum attention of customers within a set time frame.

Tenure - It is the length of the lease by which a property is held.

Title - The ultimate record of ownership of a property, the evidence of which is found in the title deeds.

Transfer Deeds - The Land Registry document that transfers legal ownership from seller to buyer.

V

Vendor - This has nothing to do with drugs, so calm down! It is the legal name sometimes used to describe the seller of the property.
