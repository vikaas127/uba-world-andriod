Connecting to the Internet:

The most common is through an ADSL line, but you can also connect through fibre-optic cable (up to six times faster than ADSL), mobile signal or even via satellite.


When choosing an Internet package??

Take these factors into account:

  • Speed of connection
  • Cost
  • Download allowances
  • Contract length
  • Location also helps as Unfortunately, you're much less likely to be able to sign up to a super-fast fibre broadband service in a remote part of the UK than you are in built-up areas.

The right combination of these things should create the best package for you.


ATTENTION ALL STUDENTS!

Student broadband packages typically feature unlimited download allowances.

What sets student broadband apart from heavy use broadband is that contracts last nine months instead of the 12-month or 18-month terms that usually apply.

However if you find that the total cost over the shorter period is more expensive than a standard 12-month deal, then it is worth going for the latter.
